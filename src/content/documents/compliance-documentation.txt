Compliance & Documentation Guide
This Compliance & Documentation Guide explains how buyers and sellers may use Trade82’s product-information fields, document library, document-storage tools, templates, official-source links, and transaction records.
Trade82 provides general workflow tools and reference materials for B2B trade. Trade82 is not a law firm, customs broker, regulatory consultant, testing laboratory, certification body, government agency, manufacturer, importer of record, exporter of record, or guarantor of product compliance.
This Guide should be read together with the Trade82 Terms of Service, applicable transaction terms, product policies, and any written agreement between the buyer and seller.
1. Trade82’s Limited Role
Trade82 may provide:
* Product-information fields
* Seller-provided compliance-information fields
* Seller-provided document-availability fields
* Trade-document templates
* Links to government or agency resources
* Private document-storage tools
* Message attachments
* Order and transaction records
* Administrative account or listing review
* General operational guidance
Unless Trade82 expressly agrees otherwise in a separate written agreement, Trade82 does not:
* Determine which laws apply to a product
* Classify a product for regulatory purposes
* Issue a certification
* Conduct laboratory testing
* Audit a manufacturing facility
* Approve an ingredient
* Approve a product label
* File a government application
* Act as a responsible person
* Act as importer or exporter of record
* Provide legal, customs, tax, safety, or regulatory advice
* Guarantee that a document is genuine
* Guarantee that a document remains valid
* Guarantee that a product may legally be sold or imported
* Guarantee acceptance by a buyer, carrier, bank, customs authority, marketplace, or government agency
The availability of a field, template, link, badge, document category, or upload function does not create such a service or guarantee.
2. User Responsibility
Each user is responsible for determining the legal, regulatory, technical, labeling, testing, documentation, licensing, and certification requirements that apply to the user’s product and transaction.
Requirements may depend on:
* Product category
* Product composition
* Intended use
* Product claims
* Consumer group
* Destination country
* State, province, or local law
* Sales channel
* Shipping method
* Importer
* Exporter
* Manufacturer
* End user
* End use
* Order quantity
* Packaging
* Labeling
* Applicable exemptions
A document suitable for one product, shipment, company, or destination may be unsuitable for another.

A seller’s selection of a document or compliance option means only that the seller represented that the information or document applies or can be provided. It does not mean that Trade82 has inspected, authenticated, certified, approved, or legally evaluated it.
4. No Automatic Compliance Determination
A product is not automatically compliant merely because:
* It is listed on Trade82
* A seller account is verified
* A product contains a certification label
* A seller selected a compliance option
* A document was uploaded
* A template was completed
* A government website was linked
* A buyer accepted a quotation
* Payment was processed
* An order was created
* A shipment passed through customs
Compliance must be evaluated based on the actual product, claims, documents, transaction, destination, and applicable law.
5. Seller Responsibilities
The seller is responsible for ensuring that product and company information submitted to Trade82 is accurate, current, complete, and not misleading.
Seller responsibilities may include:
* Confirming product classification
* Confirming ingredient or material information
* Confirming country of origin
* Confirming manufacturing information
* Confirming applicable certifications
* Maintaining current certificates
* Maintaining test reports
* Maintaining safety records
* Maintaining batch or lot records
* Confirming product claims
* Confirming labels and packaging
* Confirming trademark and distribution authority
* Confirming export eligibility
* Providing accurate commercial and shipping documents
* Informing buyers when a document expires, changes, or becomes unavailable
* Preserving original records
* Responding to regulatory or safety inquiries
* Conducting recalls or corrective action when legally required
The seller must not describe a product as approved, registered, certified, tested, compliant, organic, vegan, medical, therapeutic, clinically proven, or government-authorized unless the statement is accurate and appropriately supported.

The buyer is responsible for conducting independent due diligence before purchasing, importing, distributing, advertising, or selling a product.
Buyer responsibilities may include:
* Determining applicable import requirements
* Determining the importer of record
* Confirming local product classification
* Confirming required registrations
* Confirming required licenses
* Confirming labeling requirements
* Confirming testing requirements
* Confirming document validity
* Confirming certificate scope
* Confirming the identity of the certificate holder
* Confirming whether a certificate applies to the exact product
* Confirming the issuing laboratory or organization
* Confirming expiration dates
* Confirming product claims
* Confirming intellectual-property rights
* Confirming customs and tariff information
* Confirming marketplace requirements
* Confirming insurance requirements
* Obtaining advice from qualified professionals
Receiving a document from a seller does not remove the buyer’s obligation to evaluate whether the document is sufficient for the intended market and transaction.

14. Private Documents and Sharing
New documents uploaded through the current My Documents flow are stored as Private by default.
A document should not be considered disclosed to a buyer merely because it:
* Was uploaded
* Was placed in a folder
* Was labeled as Compliance
* Was placed in a Shared with buyer category
* Contains a buyer’s name
* Is referenced in a message
* Appears in the seller’s document workspace
A document is shared only when Trade82 expressly provides access to the intended authorized recipient through an enabled sharing function or when the user sends it through another supported Trade82 communication function.
Users must confirm that the intended recipient can access the correct document before relying on delivery.

Trade82’s technical access controls do not replace an NDA, confidentiality agreement, data-processing agreement, or other legal protection that may be appropriate.

20. Product Claims and Advertising
A certificate or test report does not automatically support every marketing statement.

* Truthful
* Not misleading
* Properly qualified
* Supported by appropriate evidence
* Consistent with the product’s legal classification
* Appropriate for the intended market
A product may be classified differently because of its intended use or claims.
Users should not convert a cosmetic, food, supplement, household product, or general consumer product into a drug, medical device, pesticide, or other regulated category through unsupported claims.
21. Cosmetics and MoCRA
For cosmetic products marketed in the United States, MoCRA includes requirements involving facility registration, product listing, serious adverse-event reporting, safety substantiation, and recordkeeping, subject to applicable definitions and exemptions. Responsibility may fall on facilities and the legally defined responsible person—not on Trade82 merely because the product is listed or sold through the platform.
Trade82 does not act as the responsible person, U.S. agent, facility registrant, product-listing filer, safety assessor, or adverse-event reporter unless expressly agreed in a separate written service agreement.
The presence of FDA-related information on a product page does not mean:
* FDA approved the cosmetic
* FDA reviewed the product’s safety
* FDA reviewed the product claims
* FDA confirmed the label
* Trade82 confirmed MoCRA compliance
* An exemption applies
The responsible parties must independently determine their obligations.

23. Import and Export Controls
A transaction may be restricted based on the product, technology, destination, purchaser, consignee, intermediary, end user, or end use.
BIS explains that end-user and end-use restrictions can create licensing requirements even where an item’s classification or destination would not otherwise require a license, and listed-party restrictions may apply to purchasers, consignees, and other transaction parties—not only the final recipient.
Users are responsible for:
* Product classification
* Export-control classification
* Destination review
* End-user review
* End-use review
* Restricted-party screening
* License determination
* License-exception eligibility
* Diversion-risk review
* Record retention
Trade82 does not guarantee that a screening result is complete or that a transaction is legally permitted.

Users should evaluate:
* Buyers
* Sellers
* Owners
* Beneficial owners
* Banks
* Payment participants
* Consignees
* Freight forwarders
* Intermediaries
* Destinations
* End users
* End uses
Trade82 may conduct its own screening or review, but any platform review does not replace the user’s independent obligations.
Trade82 may suspend, reject, cancel, delay, block, or report activity where sanctions, export-control, fraud, or other legal risks are identified.

Compliance and Documentation Process Summary
The seller enters product and compliance information, identifies documents that may be available, and stores relevant files in the seller document workspace.
The buyer reviews the available information and requests any documents necessary for its due diligence. Both parties independently verify that the documents apply to the exact product, transaction, destination, and intended use.
Trade82 may provide templates, official-source links, private storage, communication tools, and limited administrative review. Trade82 does not become the manufacturer, certification body, laboratory, responsible person, importer, exporter, customs broker, regulatory adviser, or guarantor of compliance.
