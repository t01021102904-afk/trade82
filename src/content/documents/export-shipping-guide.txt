Export & Shipping Guide
This Export & Shipping Guide explains how buyers and sellers may coordinate export, transportation, customs, delivery, and shipment records when using Trade82.
Trade82 provides a digital B2B marketplace, communication tools, payment and order records, and limited shipment-status functionality. Trade82 does not provide transportation, freight forwarding, customs clearance, warehousing, cargo inspection, or cargo insurance unless Trade82 expressly agrees to provide a specific service in a separate written agreement signed by an authorized representative.
This Guide should be read together with the Trade82 Terms of Service, applicable transaction terms, and any written agreement between the buyer and seller.
1. Trade82’s Limited Role
Trade82 is a technology platform that helps buyers and sellers discover each other, communicate, record transaction terms, process supported payments, and maintain order and shipment information.
Unless expressly stated in a separate written agreement, Trade82 is not:
* The buyer or seller of the goods
* The manufacturer of the goods
* The exporter of record
* The importer of record
* The owner of the goods
* A carrier
* A freight forwarder
* A non-vessel-operating common carrier
* An ocean transportation intermediary
* A customs broker
* A warehouse operator
* A fulfillment provider
* A cargo inspector
* A certification body
* A regulatory consultant
* A tax adviser
* An insurance company
* An issuer of shipping documents
* A guarantor of delivery or customs clearance
Trade82 does not normally take possession, custody, control, or title to goods listed or sold through the platform.
A payment processed through Trade82 does not mean that Trade82 owns, inspects, stores, exports, imports, ships, or delivers the goods.
2. No Shipping or Customs Agency Relationship
The buyer and seller are responsible for arranging transportation directly or through an independent carrier, freight forwarder, customs broker, warehouse, courier, postal service, or other logistics provider.
A shipping agreement entered into with a carrier or logistics provider is separate from the user’s agreement with Trade82.
Trade82 is not a party to that shipping agreement and does not become the agent, representative, employee, partner, subcontractor, or guarantor of the carrier or logistics provider.
When Trade82 displays information about a logistics provider, provides an external link, or introduces a user to a third-party service provider, that information or introduction does not by itself constitute:
* An endorsement
* A warranty
* A partnership
* An agency relationship
* A guarantee of pricing
* A guarantee of availability
* A guarantee of regulatory compliance
* A guarantee of delivery performance
Users must independently evaluate and contract with any third-party logistics provider.
3. Agreement Between Buyer and Seller
Before payment or shipment, the buyer and seller should clearly agree on:
* The exact product
* Quantity
* Unit and total price
* Packaging requirements
* Shipping cost
* Shipping method
* Carrier-selection responsibility
* Freight-forwarder responsibility
* Incoterm
* Named port, place, terminal, warehouse, or destination
* Export-clearance responsibility
* Import-clearance responsibility
* Duties and taxes
* Cargo-insurance responsibility
* Required shipping documents
* Production lead time
* Expected ship date
* Estimated arrival date
* Inspection requirements
* Risk-of-loss transfer
* Procedures for damaged, missing, delayed, or rejected goods
Information shown in a product listing, RFQ, message, payment request, or order record does not replace a clear agreement between the buyer and seller.
Where information conflicts, the parties should resolve the conflict in writing before payment or shipment.
4. Incoterms
Incoterms are used to clarify the tasks, costs, and risks allocated between a buyer and seller in the delivery of goods. The current version is Incoterms® 2020.
Selecting or mentioning an Incoterm on Trade82 does not automatically create a complete shipping agreement.
The buyer and seller should record:
1. The exact Incoterm
2. The exact named place or port
3. The applicable version, such as Incoterms® 2020
Example:
FOB Busan Port, South Korea — Incoterms® 2020
Not:
FOB
The parties are responsible for understanding the selected Incoterm and confirming how freight, loading, unloading, insurance, export clearance, import clearance, duties, taxes, and risk are allocated.
Trade82 does not select the correct Incoterm for the parties and does not guarantee that an Incoterm selected in a listing, RFQ, quotation, message, or order is suitable for a particular transaction.
5. Seller Responsibilities
Unless the parties expressly agree otherwise, the seller is responsible for accurately describing and preparing the goods it agrees to supply.
The seller’s responsibilities may include:
* Supplying the agreed product and quantity
* Confirming production availability
* Using packaging appropriate for the goods and agreed transportation method
* Accurately stating product origin
* Accurately stating the product description, quantity, value, weight, and dimensions
* Providing documents the seller agreed to provide
* Complying with applicable export restrictions
* Confirming whether an export license or authorization is required
* Using truthful invoices and shipping records
* Delivering the goods according to the agreed Incoterm
* Selecting qualified service providers when the seller is responsible for transportation
* Entering accurate shipment information in Trade82
* Correcting shipment information when an error is discovered
* Informing the buyer of known delays, exceptions, damage, shortages, or documentation problems
* Retaining evidence of packing, dispatch, carrier handover, and delivery when available
The seller must not:
* Enter a false shipping status
* Submit a false tracking number
* Mark an order as shipped before carrier handover
* Mark an order as delivered without a reasonable basis
* Falsify a commercial invoice
* Understate or overstate customs value
* Misstate product origin
* Use a false HS code
* Conceal regulated or hazardous contents
* Submit altered shipping documents
* Attempt to bypass export controls, sanctions, customs requirements, or import restrictions
Trade82 may restrict an account or order when shipment information appears false, misleading, unlawful, or materially inconsistent with the transaction record.
6. Buyer Responsibilities
The buyer is responsible for determining whether the goods may lawfully be imported, distributed, marketed, and sold in the destination country.
The buyer’s responsibilities may include:
* Providing a complete and accurate delivery address
* Providing the correct legal company and consignee information
* Confirming the intended destination and end use
* Determining whether the buyer or another party will act as importer of record
* Appointing a customs broker when required
* Confirming local import licenses and registrations
* Confirming product-specific regulations
* Confirming labeling requirements
* Confirming duties, taxes, tariffs, and customs fees
* Providing required importer information
* Responding to carrier and customs requests
* Paying charges allocated to the buyer
* Arranging delivery appointments when required
* Receiving and inspecting the goods
* Reporting visible damage, shortage, or incorrect goods promptly
* Preserving packaging, photographs, delivery records, and inspection evidence
* Complying with applicable sanctions, restricted-party, and end-use rules
The buyer must not provide a false delivery address, false importer information, false end-use information, or instructions intended to evade duties, taxes, sanctions, customs controls, or product regulations.

Trade82 is not responsible for differences between an estimated shipping amount and the amount ultimately charged by a carrier, freight forwarder, customs authority, warehouse, broker, or other service provider.

11. Tracking Information
A tracking number may not become active immediately after it is created.
Carrier tracking information may be delayed, unavailable, inaccurate, or incomplete because of:
* Delayed carrier scanning
* Consolidated shipments
* Handovers between carriers
* Freight-forwarder systems
* Customs holds
* Port or terminal delays
* Data-entry errors
* Carrier-system outages
* Local delivery partners
* Changes to the transportation route
Trade82 does not control carrier tracking systems and does not guarantee the availability, accuracy, or timeliness of tracking data.
A tracking number displayed on Trade82 should be checked through the relevant carrier or freight forwarder.
12. Export and Shipping Documents
The required documents depend on the goods, destination, transaction structure, carrier, and applicable law.
Common documents may include:
* Pro forma invoice
* Commercial invoice
* Packing list

* Bill of lading
* Air waybill
* Export declaration
* Export license
* Insurance certificate
* Inspection certificate
* Certificate of analysis
* Safety data sheet
* Product registration or compliance documents
* Other destination-specific documents
The U.S. International Trade Administration notes that document requirements may vary by product and destination and advises exporters to confirm required documents with the foreign customer, importer, or freight forwarder. It also distinguishes commercial invoices, packing lists, air waybills, bills of lading, export filings, and licenses.
Trade82 does not issue, sign, validate, authenticate, approve, or guarantee shipping or customs documents unless expressly stated for a specific document.
A document template provided by Trade82 is provided for general organizational assistance only. It does not guarantee that the completed document is legally sufficient or accepted by:
* A customs authority
* A bank
* A carrier
* A port
* A warehouse
* An insurer
* A government agency
* A buyer
* A seller
The responsible party must review each document before use.

Customs authorities may:
* Request additional information
* Inspect the goods
* Reclassify the goods
* Revalue the goods
* Assess additional duties or taxes
* Delay release
* Refuse entry
* Require relabeling
* Require testing
* Require permits
* Detain the shipment
* Seize the shipment
* Order return or destruction
The buyer and seller must determine which party is responsible under their contract, Incoterm, and applicable law.
Trade82 is not responsible for a customs authority’s decision, processing time, inspection, classification, valuation, duty assessment, penalty, detention, seizure, release, return, or destruction of goods.
15. Product Classification and Origin
HS codes, tariff classifications, origin statements, and customs values affect customs processing and may affect duties, taxes, licenses, quotas, and regulatory requirements.
Product information shown on Trade82 may be supplied by the seller and is not a binding customs ruling.
Trade82 does not guarantee:
* The correctness of an HS code
* Eligibility for a free-trade-agreement benefit
* The correctness of country-of-origin treatment
* The applicable tariff rate
* The acceptability of a declared customs value
* The availability of a duty exemption
* The legality of a particular import or export
The responsible party should obtain advice from a qualified customs broker, trade professional, or relevant government authority when needed.
16. Export Controls, Sanctions, and Restricted Parties
The parties are responsible for confirming whether the goods, destination, purchaser, consignee, intermediary, end user, or intended end use is subject to export controls or sanctions.
U.S. export restrictions may apply based on the end user, end use, destination, or participation of a restricted party, including circumstances where a license would not otherwise be required. BIS also directs parties to restricted-party lists and due-diligence guidance.
Users must not use Trade82 to:
* Export prohibited goods
* Transact with prohibited persons
* Conceal the final destination
* Conceal the final recipient
* Misstate the end use
* Route goods through another country to avoid restrictions
* Use false consignee information
* Evade sanctions
* Circumvent licensing requirements
* Facilitate unlawful transshipment
Trade82 may restrict, suspend, cancel, or report activity that appears unlawful or high risk. Any review performed by Trade82 does not replace the users’ own compliance obligations.

The selected Incoterm may allocate certain responsibilities, but the parties must still confirm whether the selected arrangement is legally and operationally possible in the destination country.
Trade82 does not calculate or guarantee:

20. Loss, Damage, Shortage, or Incorrect Goods
When goods appear lost, damaged, short, or incorrect, the buyer should preserve available evidence, including:
* Outer packaging
* Inner packaging
* Labels
* Photographs
* Videos
* Delivery receipt
* Carrier records
* Package count
* Weight records
* Inspection report
* Commercial invoice
* Packing list
* Message history
The buyer and seller should contact the responsible carrier, freight forwarder, warehouse, insurer, or customs broker within the deadlines applicable to that service.
Trade82 may allow the parties to exchange information or preserve platform records, but Trade82 does not inspect the goods and does not determine the physical cause of a loss.
Trade82 does not guarantee that a carrier, insurer, buyer, or seller will accept or pay a claim.
21. Delivery and Inspection
A Delivered status does not mean that the buyer has inspected or accepted the goods.
The buyer should inspect the shipment within a commercially reasonable period and in accordance with the buyer–seller agreement, carrier terms, insurance requirements, and applicable law.
The buyer should promptly notify the seller and responsible service provider of:
* Visible damage
* Missing cartons
* Quantity shortages
* Incorrect products
* Broken seals
* Temperature problems
* Packaging damage
* Suspected tampering
Trade82 does not conduct physical delivery inspections and does not certify that goods were delivered in the agreed condition.

23. Customs Holds, Abandoned Goods, Returns, and Destruction
A shipment may incur storage, demurrage, detention, return, disposal, or destruction costs when it cannot be cleared or delivered.
The responsible party will be determined by:
* The buyer–seller agreement
* The agreed Incoterm
* Carrier terms
* Customs rules
* Applicable law
* The cause of the problem
Trade82 does not accept physical returns and does not provide a warehouse or return address unless expressly agreed in writing.
No user should instruct a carrier to return goods to Trade82 without Trade82’s prior written authorization.
24. Shipment Exceptions
An Exception status may indicate a delay, hold, failed delivery, customs issue, documentation problem, damage report, route change, or another transportation problem.
The status does not identify the responsible party and does not automatically establish a right to:
* A refund
* A replacement
* Compensation
* Cancellation
* Insurance payment
* Chargeback
* Seller payout release or suspension
The buyer and seller must review the underlying facts and their agreement.
25. Refunds and Shipping Disputes
A delay, shipment exception, or tracking problem does not automatically require a full refund.
Refund eligibility may depend on:
* Whether the goods were shipped
* The agreed Incoterm
* The agreed delivery obligation
* The reason for the delay
* Whether the buyer provided correct information
* Whether customs clearance was completed
* Whether the goods were returned
* Whether the seller breached the agreed terms
* Whether a carrier or insurer accepted a claim
* Applicable law

Trade82 may request transaction, shipment, message, tracking, or document records when reviewing a dispute.
Any review or assistance provided by Trade82 does not mean that Trade82 caused the shipping problem, accepted responsibility for the shipment, or guaranteed recovery of funds.

Export and Shipping Process Summary
The buyer and seller agree on the product, shipping cost, Incoterm, destination, required documents, and other commercial terms.
The party responsible for transportation selects and contracts with an independent carrier or logistics provider. The seller prepares the goods and documents according to the parties’ agreement. After dispatch, the seller may enter carrier, tracking, shipment-reference, and status information in Trade82.
The buyer can review the available shipment information through the order page. All shipment information remains informational and should be verified with the responsible carrier or logistics provider.
The buyer and seller remain responsible for export compliance, import compliance, customs clearance, duties, taxes, insurance, inspections, and claims. Trade82 provides platform records and communication tools but does not become the carrier, exporter, importer, customs broker, warehouse, insurer, or guarantor of the shipment.
