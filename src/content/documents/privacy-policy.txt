3. Information We Collect

The information processed depends on the features used and the stage of a transaction.

3.1 Account and Authentication Information

• Clerk user identifier
• name, display name, email address, and profile image
• account role, such as seller, buyer, both, or administrator
• language preference
• sign-in, session, authentication status, and security events
• authentication information supplied through Clerk when Google Sign-In is selected

Trade82 does not collect or store a user’s Google password.

3.2 Personal and Professional Profile Information

• company affiliation, job title, department, and biography
• phone number and LinkedIn URL
• country and city
• other business information the user adds to a profile

3.3 Company and Verification Information

• legal name, trade name, and English display name
• seller or buyer role, website, country, state or province, city, and business address
• descriptions, categories, and logos
• representative name and Korean business registration number
• export experience, export countries, certifications, and manufacturer or distributor status
• verification requests, status, results, and administrator notes
• filenames, storage paths, and contents of documents submitted for verification

A verification status reflects only the limited platform review performed. It is not a guarantee of product safety, regulatory compliance, financial condition, manufacturing capacity, or the authenticity of every representation or document.

3.4 Seller and Buyer Business Information

Seller profile information may include product categories, minimum order quantities, lead times, certifications, shipping terms, payment terms, and manufacturing or distribution information.

Buyer profile information may include buyer type, purchasing categories, preferred supplier type, target order size, monthly import volume, import experience, purchase timeline, and sales channels.

3.5 Product and Public Marketplace Information

• product names and English names
• product images and image metadata
• categories, tags, and descriptions
• price ranges, currency, and pricing units
• minimum order quantities, lead times, sample availability, and private-label availability
• production capacity, origin, and shipping origin
• Incoterms and HS codes
• ingredients or materials, packaging, storage, and shelf life
• certifications, regulatory or compliance claims, and available documents
• buyer notes, visibility controls, views, saves, and listing status

Company, product, review, image, and document information that a user publishes may be visible to unauthenticated visitors and search engines.

3.6 Inquiries, RFQs, Quotations, and Messages

• buyer, seller, recipient company, and relevant contacts
• product, message, quantity, and target date
• RFQ product name, category, sourcing type and purpose
• quantity, target price, currency, trade terms, and destination country
• shape, capacity, material, certification, features, and target delivery date
• quotations, MOQ, lead time, Incoterms, sample or OEM availability
• messages, attachments, timestamps, and conversation status

Messages and attachments are not public, but they may be accessible to the participating users and companies, authorized Trade82 administrators, and infrastructure providers that need access to provide the Service.

3.7 Payment, Order, Refund, and Dispute Information

Trade82 may process:

• order number, products, quantity, price, and transaction terms
• product amount, shipping amount, gross amount, platform fee, and seller payable amount
• currency and payment deadline
• Stripe customer, Checkout Session, Payment Intent, and Charge identifiers
• payment status and timestamps
• refund amounts and status
• dispute or chargeback identifiers, reason, and status
• payment, refund, dispute, and administrator audit events

Payment card numbers, CVCs, and complete card credentials are entered directly into Stripe’s payment environment and are not designed to be collected or stored by Trade82. Trade82 may retain transaction identifiers, amounts, status, and information needed for fraud, refund, and dispute handling.

3.8 Order Contact and Shipping Information

Transaction snapshots may include:

• buyer and seller company names and contact names
• email addresses and phone numbers
• countries and addresses
• origin and destination
• carrier and freight forwarder names
• tracking, bill of lading, air waybill, and shipment reference numbers
• ship dates, estimated arrival dates, status, and notes

Some transaction snapshots may remain after a profile is changed or an account is closed to preserve historical, accounting, dispute, and audit records.

3.9 Seller Payout Information

• country, bank, and branch
• account holder
• account number
• account type, bank code, and SWIFT or BIC
• bank and beneficiary addresses
• payout and supported currencies
• intermediary bank information
• payout memo, external transfer reference, and payout proof

Complete account numbers are designed to be stored using application-level encryption and normally displayed only in masked form or by the last four digits. Authorized administrators may access full details only when needed for payout processing, audit, security investigation, or legal compliance. Necessary information may also be provided to the bank or financial institution completing the transfer.

3.10 Uploaded Files

Uploads may include product images, company logos, verification documents, certificates, test reports, contracts, trade documents, buyer-shared files, message attachments, and payout proof.

Public files may be visible to anyone. Restricted files are designed to use role and ownership checks and time-limited signed access. Users should not upload unnecessary government identifiers, passport information, medical information, personal banking passwords, government login credentials, or sensitive information belonging to another person.

3.11 Technical Information Collected Automatically

• IP address
• browser, device, and operating system information
• request path, time, and response status
• session identifiers and essential authentication cookies
• server, security, error, and rate-limit logs
• authentication, administrator, and transaction event records
• diagnostic information required for reliability, fraud prevention, and troubleshooting

The current Trade82 source code does not include an advertising behavioral tracking SDK or a third-party behavioral analytics SDK. Before adding such technology, Trade82 will update this Policy and implement any notice, consent, or opt-out mechanism required by law.

3.12 Support and Other Sources

Trade82 may receive information from support emails, counterparties involved in an order, public business websites or official sources used for verification, and payment or security providers that provide legally permitted fraud and risk signals.

12. Retention

Trade82 applies the following retention criteria:

• Account, profile, company, and product information: while the account or company profile remains active, followed by review of deletion, de-identification, and legal exceptions after closure.
• Verification requests and documents: for the period necessary to review, reverify, prevent fraud, and address challenges, then deleted or access-restricted when no longer needed.
• Inquiries, RFQs, quotations, messages, and attachments: for the business conversation; records not connected to a transaction are generally retained for up to three years after the last activity, while transaction or dispute records follow the transaction period.
• Orders, payments, refunds, disputes, shipping, payouts, and audit records: generally up to seven years after the transaction closes, or longer when required by accounting, tax, anti-fraud, limitations, or other law.
• Security, access, and error logs: generally up to twelve months, longer when connected to an actual or suspected incident, fraud, dispute, or legal hold.
• Support records: generally up to three years after closure.
• Consent records: as needed to establish consent and withdrawal.
• Backups: information may remain for a limited additional period in encrypted backups and is overwritten through normal backup cycles. Backup information is generally used only for restoration, security, or legal preservation.

Deletion may be limited while a legal obligation, dispute, fraud review, payout, refund, chargeback, or legal claim remains open. When retention is no longer necessary, information is deleted or de-identified using reasonable methods.

13. Security

Trade82’s current design includes:

• Clerk authentication and session management;
• role, company-ownership, and administrator authorization checks;
• server-side access control and API validation;
• rate limits on sensitive write operations;
• private storage and time-limited signed file access;
• application-level encryption and masking of payout account numbers;
• financial and administrative audit events;
• encryption in transit;
• separation of production secrets from source code;
• separation of public listings from restricted records; and

No online system can guarantee absolute security. Users should protect their accounts and promptly report suspected account or email compromise.

File type, size, and access checks may be used, but Trade82 does not guarantee that every uploaded file receives complete malware scanning. Users should independently scan untrusted attachments.
